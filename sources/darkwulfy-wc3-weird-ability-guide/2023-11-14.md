<div id="post_message_1026827" style="max-width:100%; max-height:30000px; overflow:auto">

<a href="/web/20180130181746/http://www.wc3c.net/attachment.php?attachmentid=37598&amp;stc=1&amp;d=1223115530"><embed src="/web/20180130181746im_/http://www.wc3c.net/attachment.php?attachmentid=37598&amp;stc=1&amp;d=1223115530&amp;thumb=1" /></a><br />
<br />
No, this is not a ripoff of the famed <a href="/web/20180130181746/http://www.wc3c.net/showthread.php?t=81742">Warcraft III Ability Guide</a>. One could think of it as an extension.<br />
<br />
This, rather, is a list of the "weird" side of Warcraft III abilities. As many of us know, Blizzard did some pretty weird things with their spells. This guide sets out to find and bring to light all the weird things they've done, be they helpful, hurtful, or just stupid/useless.<br />
<br />
First, I want to thank everyone who contributed to the "Weird things with Blizzard Abilities" thread I started many months ago, and got this list started:
<ul>
<li>Midiway</li>
<li>Moyack</li>

<li>Hydrolisk</li>

</ul>
<br />
<br />
Special thanks to:
<ul>
<li>Anopob for insane amounts of testing regarding many abilities, including War Club, Dark Portal, and Rain of Chaos (Archimonde).</li>
<li>Kyrbi0 for lots of testing as well</li>
<li>PitzerMike for approving this tutorial and add his super-awesome smiley to the logo.</li>
</ul>
And now, onto the Carnival!<br />
<br />
<strong>Requested Abilities</strong>
<ul>
<li>None right now</li>
</ul>
<br />
<strong>To-Do:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Add the rawcode ID's for all the spells (joy.)</li>

<br />
<strong>Note:</strong> This list is for weird things with SPELLS/ABILITIES, and only oddities that can cause issues or are confusing (such as incorrect/weird fields). The Warcraft III Ability Guide is much like this, except it explains how to use the oddities of certain spells to your advantage. This list is so people know what spells can be troublesome. It also has some of the lesser-known fields/effects some spells have, be they good or bad.<br />
<br />
<strong>General Oddities</strong><br />
<br />
This is for things that apply to most/all spells.
<ul>
<li>Many times, <strong>Targets Allowed</strong> is hardcoded into Wc3, so modifying them may do nothing.</li>
<li>Making a custom spell then setting its buff field(s) to nothing will make it default to the Blizzard buff.</li>
</ul>
<br />
<br />
<strong>Individual Spells</strong><br />
<br />
This little (not anymore!) section is for individual spells that have a lot of oddities.<br />
<br />
<strong><span class="underline">Channel</span></strong><br />
Probably one of the weirdest spells Blizzard provides us.<br />

<div style="margin:20px; margin-top:5px">
<div class="smallfont" style="margin-bottom:2px">
Table:
</div>
<div style="width:100%; overflow:auto;">
<table>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Data - Options</strong></td>
<td>Abilities based on Channel will not show up unless this field has the "Visible" box checked off.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Channel - Follow Through Time</strong></td>
<td>The maximum amount of time the spell can be channeled for. Setting it to 0 makes it a normal spell; anything above it makes it a channeling spell.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Art - Missile</strong></td>
<td>Does not work for Channel (big bummer!)<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Data - Targeting Options</strong></td>
<td>Channel has multiple types of targeting options, as well as a "Targeting Image", which brings up a circle (like Flame Strike or Blizzard). Can be useful! (<strong>Thanks Themerion</strong>)<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>
</div>

<br />
<strong><span class="underline">Shadow Strike</span></strong><br />
A simple ability but it has a few weird fields.<br />

<div style="margin:20px; margin-top:5px">
<div class="smallfont" style="margin-bottom:2px">
Table:
</div>
<div style="width:100%; overflow:auto;">
<table>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Decaying Damage</strong></td>
<td>How much damage the target takes per interval after the initial damage.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Casting Time</strong></td>
<td>Not actually the casting time. It's the interval for <strong>Decaying Damage</strong>.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Decaying Power</strong></td>
<td>I don't know what it does yet does yet.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>

<br />
<strong><span class="underline">Thunder Clap, War Stomp, and Shockwave</span></strong><br />
Three spells that all deform terrain.<br />

<div style="margin:20px; margin-top:5px">
<div class="smallfont" style="margin-bottom:2px">
Table:
</div>
<div style="width:100%; overflow:auto;">
<table>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Data - Terrain Deforming</strong></td>
<td>War Stomp (Neutral Hostile 1/2/3) has this field, which could come in handy. (<strong>Thanks Burning Rose</strong><br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Other</strong></td>
<td>These spells will always cause the ground to warp when cast, even if you change the missile art, etc.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Other</strong></td>
<td>These spells can cause Desyncs (due to the deformed terrain) if you're checking for Z height in triggers.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>

<br />
<br />
<br />
<strong><span class="underline">Cluster Rockets</span></strong><br />
<strong>(Thanks so much IgnitedStar!!)</strong><br />
A spell that is known to have quite the array of weird fields and abnormalties.<br />

<div style="margin:20px; margin-top:5px">
<div class="smallfont" style="margin-bottom:2px">
Table:
</div>
<div style="width:100%; overflow:auto;">
<table>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Damage Amount</strong></td>
<td>Amount of damage that is dealt per <strong>Damage Interval</strong>.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Damage Interval</strong></td>
<td>Amount of time per when <strong>Damage Amount</strong> is applied.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Effect Duration</strong></td>
<td>The amount of time <strong>Damage Interval</strong> is accounted for before it stops checking every X seconds, X coming from <strong>Damage Interval</strong>. Or... This might also be the amount of damage any one <strong>Damage Interval</strong> can deal to all units within the <strong>Area of Effect</strong>.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Max Damage</strong></td>
<td>The maximum amount of damage any one unit can take from a single casting of the spell. Or... This might also be the amount of damage any one <strong>Damage Invertal</strong> can deal.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Missle Count</strong></td>
<td>Eyecandy only. Determines how many missiles are shown, so if there are more missiles than the duration lasts, missiles will be shown w/o damage, and vice versa. The only way to determine the right timing is by guess + check.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Area of Effect</strong></td>
<td>This goes hand in hand with <strong>Missle Count</strong>. Even if I extended the <strong>Range</strong>, I wasn't able to change where the missles landed or how they landed. A test with a large area showed that the missle art is truly just for effect: Even if you use an area of a thousand, the missles will only cover a small area of that thousand, but all units within the <strong>Area of Effect</strong> will still get hit.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Spell Duration Hero/Unit</strong></td>
<td>This specifies the amount of stun dealt as units get hit according to <strong>Damage Interval</strong>. This only happens once, as if the unit is hit at any <strong>Damage Interval</strong>. So, even if a unit gets hit by Cluster Rockets late, it will still be stunned for this amount of time. The same holds true for units that are hit within the area. Blizzard made it so that the stun ended just as the <strong>Effect Duration ended</strong>.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Cooldown</strong></td>
<td>I feel a need to put <strong>Cooldown</strong> in here. Cluster Rockets is a channeling spell; if the <strong>Effect Duration</strong> surpasses the <strong>Cooldown</strong>, then Cluster Rockets can literally run forever with sufficient mana.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td>(Thanks Burning Rose:)<br />
</td>
<td></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Buffs</strong></td>
<td>I've found that if you give the <strong>Buff</strong> field for Cluster Rockets (Which normally has 'Cluster Rockets', not 'Stunned') the 'Stunned' buff, it doesn't work; the stun doesn't go off. If you, instead, give it a buff based off Cluster Rockets, the stun works, but the damage doesn't. However, the Cluster Rockets buff looks just like the stunned buff, so it doesn't matter as long as you leave it that way.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td>(Thanks TKF:)<br />
</td>
<td></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Art - Projectile Speed</strong></td>
<td>Does not affect Cluster Rockets.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Other</strong></td>
<td>Rockets take approx. .75-.79 seconds to reach their target, regardless of speed or distance. (So if the range is really long, the rockets will go insanely fast, and vice versa)<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>
</div>

<br />
<strong><span class="underline">Flame Strike</span></strong><br />
Flame Strike's fields are very simple. However, because of the way Blizzard made Flame Strike's default fields, it can be very confusing (For example; the spell's default tooltip says "45 damage per second", but the Full Damage field for level 1 is 15.)<br />
<strong>Thanks IgnitedStar for Flame Strike's fields!</strong><br />

<div style="margin:20px; margin-top:5px">
<div class="smallfont" style="margin-bottom:2px">
Table:
</div>
<div style="width:100%; overflow:auto;">
<table>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Full Damage Dealt</strong></td>
<td>This is the amount of damage dealt per <strong>Full Damage Interval</strong>.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Full Damage Interval</strong></td>
<td>This is the amount of time <strong>Full Damage Dealt</strong> deals its damage. (It's .33 by default.)<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Half Damage Dealt</strong></td>
<td>This is the amount of damage the leftover fires deal.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Half Damage Interval</strong></td>
<td>This one is the amount of time <strong>Half Damage Dealt deals is damage</strong>. (It's 1 by default)<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Maximum Damage</strong></td>
<td>I'm 75% sure that this is the maximum amount of damage any unit can sustain from <strong>Full Damage</strong>. This might also count for <strong>Half Damage</strong>, but I doubt it.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Casting Time</strong></td>
<td>For Flame Strike, this is the amount of time it takes for the whole spell to fire off.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td>(Thanks TKF:)<br />
</td>
<td></td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Duration Hero/Unit</strong></td>
<td>Hero determines how long the main blaze burns, Unit determines the length of the afterburn.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Other</strong></td>
<td>Flame Strikes do not stack damage; the first will always do the damaging, even if the second does more damage.<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>

<br />
<strong><span class="underline">Gold Mines</span></strong><br />
Apparently there's some stuff with Gold mines...<br />
<span class="underline">Note:</span> At times, and seemingly randomly, units who enter/leave gold mines (eg: Peasants + Orcs) will suddenly move extremely slowly. This is because if your town hall is very close to the gold mine, it attempts to "balance" this by slowing the peasants. Re-ordering them will fix this temporarily. (<strong>Thanks Anopob for that</strong>)<br />
<br />
<strong>Thanks MCV for testing Undead (Blighted) Gold Mines.</strong><br />

<div style="margin:20px; margin-top:5px">
<div class="smallfont" style="margin-bottom:2px">
Table:
</div>
<div style="width:100%; overflow:auto;">
<table>
<tbody>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Building Mines</strong></td>
<td>The unit that builds a Blighted Gold Mine must be Undead<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="even">
<td><strong>Targets</strong></td>
<td>Only works on Undead structures and anything else will make it contain 0 gold.<br />
</td>
</tr>
<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Gather Gold (Acolyte)</strong></td>
<td>The unit that has the this ability must be Undead<br />
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<strong>Thanks TKF for testing the Gold Mine Ability.</strong><br />

<div style="width:100%; overflow:auto;">
<table>
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 50%" />

<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Gold Mine (Ability)</strong></td>
<td>By adding the Gold Mine ability to a unit...:
<ul>
<li>It will appear as a big yellow dot on the map</li>
<li>Damage and Gold amount will clash on the UI, so only Gold amount will be shown. Using a second attack will show the second attack.</li>
<li>The unit can be harvested from, and will die indefinitely when it runs out of gold.</li>
</ul></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

Table:

<div style="width:100%; overflow:auto;">
<table>

<tr class="odd">
<td><strong>Gold</strong></td>
<td>Spawns an empty gold mine. Gold cannot be gathered unless added first (by triggers) and then harvested from. Removing gold via triggers will display "-(amount)". It will collapse after one gathering afterwards.<br />
</td>
